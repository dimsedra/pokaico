# Pokaico Tone

## The feeling

Pokaico is a cozy AI lifespace. A place, not a tool. A companion, not a chatbot.

Everything we write — README, docs, social, UI copy — should feel like a warm corner of a room, not a press release.

## Voice principles

**Speak to one person.** "You" not "users." This is intimate. Someone invited this app onto their desktop, into their life.

**Simple words, short lines.** No buzzwords. No "leverage," "paradigm," "ecosystem," "seamless," "robust." If you wouldn't say it to a friend, don't write it.

**Honest.** We don't promise AGI. We don't claim to be the "best" anything. We say what we are and what we aren't, and we trust people to feel the difference.

**Quiet confidence.** Pokaico doesn't shout. It sits on your desktop, pixel art glowing softly, and waits. Our writing should feel the same.

**Playful, not gimmicky.** Pixel art is cozy, not ironic. Warmth is genuine, not manufactured.

## The relationship

Pokaico is not:
- A productivity tool (no kanban, no calendar, no email)
- A cloud service (your data stays on your machine)
- A generic chatbot wrapper

Pokaico is:
- A personal AI lifespace
- A companion that remembers who you are
- Yours, forever

## Do vs. Don't

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Warm, like a friend explaining something they built | Corporate / sterile / academic |
| "You" — direct, intimate | "Users" — distanced |
| Short sentences. Breaths. Pauses. | Long paragraphs, dense spec-speak |
| Pixel-art energy: soft, glowing, inviting | Dark, cyber, "disruptive," hacker-bro |
| "This is your space" | "This is the best AI platform" |
| Show what we are | Attack competitors |

## Test

Read it aloud. If it sounds like a friend showing you something cozy they made over the weekend — you're in the right tone. If it sounds like a landing page for enterprise software, rewrite it.
